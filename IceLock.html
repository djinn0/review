Design

An ice lock is a simple time release mechanism used for self bondage. It has 2 parts a cylinder and a pin. The cylinder is filled with water and then the pin is inserted and the lock is frozen. The frozen water locks the pin in place preventing it from moving until the ice melts. The pin has holes which help the ice grip the pin better. There is a dranage hole in the side of the lock that allows excess water to flow out when the pin is inserted. There are rings on the bottom of the cylinder and on the pin that act as attachment points for restraints. Larger cylinders take longer to melt allowing for different release times based on size.

Material & Quality

The ice locks are machined from stainless steel. Stainless steel is an excellent material for an ice lock, as it does not rust, it is body safe, and it is a reasonable heat conductor so the ice will melt at a predictable rate. The ice locks are of reasonably good quality for a small manufacturer. They are very robust and durable. There are machining marks and welds that show that the ice locks are hand made, but the welds are small a neat and the steel has been nicely polished.
